About the conference

Parallel kinematic mechanisms, from theory to practice

World Parallel, also known as Parallel, is the flagship technical meeting dedicated to parallel kinematic mechanisms (PKMs) and related closed‑chain and hybrid architectures. The conference brings together researchers and practitioners from around the world working on the theory, design, modelling, control, manufacturing, and applications of parallel mechanisms in areas such as machining, medical and surgical robotics, haptics, aerospace, construction, and beyond.

Following the tradition of the 2002 Québec, 2008 Montpellier, 2014 Tianjin, and 2020 Belfast editions, held at six‑year intervals, the 2026 meeting will take place in Abu Dhabi, UAE, in a hybrid format, with participation available both in person and online. The program will feature peer‑reviewed papers, invited keynotes, tutorials, workshops, industrial demonstrations, a student forum, and special sessions on emerging topics such as reconfigurable and multi‑mode mechanisms and cable‑driven parallel robots.

Abu Dhabi — a hub for innovation and culture. Abu Dhabi is at the forefront of robotics and artificial intelligence, supported by a $4B national AI strategy aiming to become the world's first fully AI‑native government by 2027. Institutions such as Khalifa University, Mohamed bin Zayed University of Artificial Intelligence (MBZUAI), the Advanced Technology Research Council, and NYU Abu Dhabi are advancing cutting‑edge work in robotics, autonomous systems, and AI‑driven applications. Beyond technology, Abu Dhabi offers a rich cultural experience through Louvre Abu Dhabi, Manarat Al Saadiyat, Abu Dhabi Art, Sorbonne Abu Dhabi, and NYU Abu Dhabi's arts programs, making the city a vibrant meeting point of science, innovation, and heritage.

Call for papers

Topics & important dates

Peer‑reviewed contributions across the full breadth of parallel robotics theory and application.

Topics of interest

  • Core theory: architecture & type synthesis, kinematics, dynamics, stiffness/accuracy
  • Methods: control & optimization, AI/data‑driven modeling, digital twins
  • Mechanism classes: reconfigurable/metamorphic, CDPRs, hybrid serial–parallel, soft/continuum, over/under‑constrained mechanisms
  • Sensing & calibration: vision/servoing, sensor fusion, embedded metrology
  • Applications: machining, medical/surgical (RCM), micro/meso systems, haptics, construction, space/underwater, agriculture, inspection/maintenance
  • Practice & impact: HRI & safety, standards/benchmarks, open datasets, education & reproducibility

Submission & publication

  • Single blind peer review by at least two experts.
  • Paper categories: Full (6–12 pages) and Short/Industry/Position (4 pages).
  • Proceedings: All accepted and presented full papers will be published in the Springer Mechanism and Machine Science series, indexed by Scopus and Ei Compendex.
  • At least one author must register and present in person unless explicitly approved for remote presentation.
  • Word templates are provided on the EasyChair system.

Rules

  • Workshop accepted papers must have at least one full registration by the camera‑ready deadline.
  • Workshop presentation is expected unless a remote option is approved by the organizers.
  • Workshop registration includes access to technical sessions, networking events, coffee breaks, lunches and electronic proceedings.
  • Workshop pass includes networking events and city visits.
  • Workshop/tutorial registration covers the selected day only.
